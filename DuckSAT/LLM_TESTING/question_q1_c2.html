SAT Geometry Question 1

Question:

In the diagram described, PT is tangent to the circle at T, and PAB is a secant through the circle with A between P and B. If PA = 6, PB = 24, and OT = 5, what is the length of OP? A) 10 B) 12 C) 13 D) 18

Explanation:

- Because PT is tangent to the circle at T and PAB is a secant through the circle, the tangent-secant theorem (power of a point) applies: PT^2 = PA × PB. - Compute PT: PT^2 = 6 × 24 = 144, so PT = 12. - The radius to the point of tangency is perpendicular to the tangent line, so triangle OTP is a right triangle with legs OT = 5 and PT = 12. - Use the Pythagorean theorem to find OP: OP^2 = OT^2 + PT^2 = 5^2 + 12^2 = 25 + 144 = 169, so OP = 13.

Answers:

C) 13
